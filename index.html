About Me

Welcome! I am a Ph.D. Candidate in Public Policy at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. My academic research is driven by over 17 years of on-the-ground experience in peacebuilding, often in active war zones throughout my native Colombia.

Working directly with victims of conflict and participating in three peace negotiations forged in me a profound commitment to protecting human dignity, independent of any political or economic affiliation. This drive to understand and prevent violence in post-conflict settings led me back to academia as a Rotary Peace Fellow (2019-2021). This path builds on a long career with local NGOs, the Colombian Ombudsman's Office, USAID, and the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP).

My research focuses on peace consolidation, international security, and the political economy of post-conflict societies, with a regional expertise in Latin America and American foreign policy. My work has been published in the Journal of Conflict Resolution and Conflict, Security & Development.

Scenes from fieldwork in the Pacific region of Colombia. My research and teaching are dedicated to understanding and improving the conditions for those most affected by conflict.
